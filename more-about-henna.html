All you need to know about henna

Henna Facts

  • Henna is a natural dye made from the leaves of the henna plant.
  • It has been used for centuries for body art and hair dyeing.
  • Henna is known for its cooling properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
  • The color of henna can vary from orange to dark brown depending on the quality and application.

About Henna

Henna’s symbolism extends beyond beauty and decoration, serving as an important element in cultural rituals and ceremonies. In Indian weddings, for example, the bride’s hands and feet are adorned with elaborate henna patterns, often including hidden initials or symbols related to the couple. The practice is more than ornamental—henna is believed to bring good fortune, happiness, and protect against the "evil eye." The richness of the stain is also said to represent the strength of love between the couple, with darker stains traditionally associated with stronger bonds. In addition to weddings, henna is used during festivals such as Eid, Diwali, and Karva Chauth. These celebrations often include family gatherings where henna is applied, symbolizing joy and unity. In Moroccan and Middle Eastern traditions, henna is also used for protection during life-changing events such as childbirth and religious festivals. Henna designs vary by region, with Indian patterns typically involving detailed, swirling motifs, while Arabic designs focus on floral, flowing patterns that are often more spaced out. African henna designs tend to be bold and geometric. This diversity of styles reflects the versatility of henna as both a cultural and artistic expression. Today, henna is embraced globally, blending ancient traditions with modern fashion and self-expression.
